Call for speakers

 Voir la version française de l'appel à conférenciers

AFUP, the French Association of PHP Users, is pleased to announce that the 2013 edition of its national event, the Forum PHP, will be held on november 21th and 22th in Paris.

To celebrate the 200 millionth website using PHP in 2013, the AFUP has chosen as a theme for this edition: "PHP, leader on the web!" and four tracks to illustrate the strength of the PHP language running over 78% of the main websites:

  • Web giants using PHP
  • Innovation in PHP
  • Which PHP solution for my project ?
  • PHP, feedback from the trenches

Web giants using PHP

PHP is the technology that runs major websites such as : Facebook, Wikipedia, Flickr, Yahoo Answers, Delicious, Digg, SourceForce, Etsy... and most high-traffic media websites in France.
The speakers of this track will present the organizational and technical challenges they had to face to sustain the load and succeed on PHP at the scale of giants.

Innovation in PHP

PHP might soon be 20 years old, it is still evolving at high-speed: Traits, Composer, PSR, ZendCache, Built-in web server, Closures, Hip-Hop... These are just a few of the many recent innovations developed by the community.
The speakers of this track will help you understand how to use these latest innovations in PHP and its ecosystem and get maximal benefits on your projects.

Which PHP open-source solution for my project ?

PHP is the language used by the leading open-source projects on the web: Drupal, Wordpress, Symfony, Zend Framework, Magento, Prestashop, RBS Change... and many more! To start your project, the choice is hard.
The speakers of this track will share their experience implementing and using on a day-to-day basis these solutions, to help you forge your opinion and debate on the different solutions.

PHP, feedback from the trenches

There are many people using PHP, from the teenager on his blog to the web giant. The speakers of this track will share their experience on their technical and organizational challenges faced on their projects, hoping to make you save the precious time they spent finding their solutions.

Are you expert for one of those specific PHP related domains? Did you deploy one or several PHP applications (CMS, e-commerce, CRM, EDMS) in a particular context (heavy load, famous customer, innovating project)? Are you taking part in an Open Source project? Come and share your experience!

Speeches organization

The Forum is mainly constituted by conferences and workshops throughout the two days, possibly completed by other informal speeches.

Conferences should last 45 minutes, followed by a Q&A session.

Workshops take place in smaller rooms to offer more interaction. It should last up to 2 hours.

Selection process

Proposals will be reviewed after the deadline has passed. Additional information may be asked where needed. Proposals will be chosen according to their layout, relevancy for a professional audience and the overall homegeneity of the forum. Every candidate will be personnally notified of the status of their proposal. The decision is final.
